How should the "Next Moves" strip behave?

This is the centerpiece of Option C — the part that tells you what to do. Three behavior models below. Click one.

A

Status chips — auto-discovered, click to act

A row of 3-6 chips populated by simple rules: "POST ready on X" when charge ≥100%, "Buy Sock Puppet ×N" when affordable, "DEPICT: Heart of Texas affordable", "Synergy: Wedge Content prereqs met". Click a chip → it executes the action (POST fires, asset bought, drawer opens). No ranking, just whatever's actionable right now.

⚡ NEXT MOVES — 4 actionable right now

Pro: dead simple, no judgment required, just "what's possible right now." One click to act. Con: doesn't recommend strategy — chip for "buy Sock Puppet" appears even when buying paradigm projects is the bigger win.

B

Ranked recommendations — actual strategy hints

Top 3 chips, ranked by impact heuristic (e.g. "this purchase has best $/sec ratio" or "this unlocks a synergy" or "this is closest to a milestone bonus"). Color-coded by impact tier (gold/silver/bronze). The recommendations explain themselves on hover ("Hundred Hands milestone in 47 puppets, +5% all att").

⚡ NEXT MOVES — ranked by impact

Pro: solves "no direction" — actually tells you what's strategically best. Con: the heuristic has to be right, otherwise it lies. Requires writing the ranking logic (genuine design + tuning work).

C

Goal tracker — progress toward the next milestone

Instead of "do this now" chips, shows 1-3 progress bars toward the next meaningful unlock: "Synergy: Wedge Content — 38K/50K engagement", "Tier 2 in Polarization — 7/10 levels", "Mebro reveal — 80% cure". Less "what to click" and more "what you're working toward."

Combines B and C. Left half shows 1-2 goals with progress bars (the "what you're working toward"). Right half shows 2-3 click-to-act chips (the "what's possible right now"). Tells you both the strategic direction and the immediate action.
