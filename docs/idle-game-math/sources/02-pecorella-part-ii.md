# Pecorella — *The Math of Idle Games, Part II*

**Source:** https://www.gamedeveloper.com/game-platforms/the-math-of-idle-games-part-ii
**Author:** Anthony Pecorella
**Original publish:** Dec 14 2016 (Kongregate blog, mirrored on Game Developer)
**One-line summary:** Alternative growth models beyond the standard Cookie-Clicker pattern, and the mechanics that keep older generators relevant late-game.

---

## The "derivative chain" growth model

Instead of every generator producing the same currency, generators produce **other generators**, cascading. The derivative model:

> "Gen 1 is the rate at which currency is generated. Gen 2 is the rate at which Gen 1 is generated, and so on."

This produces sub-exponential growth at any finite tier count, but with $n$ tiers approaches:
$$1 + x + \frac{x^2}{2} + \frac{x^3}{6} + \cdots + \frac{x^n}{n!} \xrightarrow{n \to \infty} e^x$$

The total production converges toward exponential growth as tier count rises, but is dramatically gentler than the pure-exponential Cookie Clicker model at any single moment.

### Real-game example
**Derivative Clicker** uses exactly this — cost formula $5 \times 1.1^n$ for each tier.

---

## Generator relevance problem (and solutions)

Pecorella names the problem explicitly:

> "As you gain more tiers you get to see the lower tiers get truly huge — why would I ever buy more?"

In a derivative chain, lower tiers eventually accumulate enormous owned counts via cascading production. The manual purchase decision for a Tier-1 generator becomes meaningless when you already own billions of them auto-generated by higher tiers.

### The "purchased vs owned" split

> "Separate the **owned** count from the **purchased** count. Costs scale on purchased only. Tier-boost multipliers are also driven by purchased only."

Mechanically:
- `owned[tier]` = total instances accumulated (from auto-generation by higher tiers)
- `purchased[tier]` = how many you actively bought
- `cost(tier, n) = base × growth^purchased[tier]` — purchased drives cost, not owned
- Each purchased Gen-1 unit boosts all Gen-1 production by some fixed % (e.g. 0.05% in Derivative Clicker)

### Milestone multipliers (sometimes called "every 25 owned doubles")
A discrete-bonus pattern: at owned counts of 25, 50, 100, 200, 300, 400, 500, … grant a permanent 2× (or other-factor) multiplier to that generator. Adopted in AdVenture Capitalist, Cookie Clicker (the "1 million baked" milestones), etc.

The combination keeps every tier mechanically meaningful — players keep wanting to manually buy lower tiers for the milestone bonuses and the tier-boost multipliers, even when their auto-generated counts have gone exponential.

## Named patterns

| Pattern | Description |
|---|---|
| **Derivative chain** | Each tier is the derivative of the next; gives polynomial growth from any single tier, asymptotically exponential overall |
| **Purchased vs owned split** | Decouples cost / multiplier basis (purchased) from production basis (owned) |
| **Milestone multipliers** | Permanent step-function bonuses at owned-count thresholds keep older tiers relevant |
| **Tier-boost percentage** | Each purchased unit of a tier multiplies all units of that tier (e.g. 0.05% per purchased) |

## Named anti-patterns

| Anti-pattern | Symptom |
|---|---|
| **No counter to newest-tier dominance** | Old generators irrelevant; whole game collapses to "buy the latest thing" |
| **Costs tied to total owned in a derivative chain** | After tier-1 auto-accumulates to billions, manual buys are prohibitively expensive; player can't engage |
| **Pure-exponential overall growth** | Numbers run out of headroom too fast |

## Real-game constants

### Derivative Clicker
- Cost growth: $5 \times 1.1^n$ per generator
- Derivative chain structure
- Tier-boost: 0.05% per purchased Gen-1 boosts all Gen-1 production

### Shark Game
- Generators produce other generators (Nurse Sharks produce Sharks that harvest primary resource)
- Same derivative-chain shape

## Tunable knobs Part II adds to Part I's vocabulary

- **Tier-boost percentage** per purchased unit (typical: 0.01% – 1%)
- **Milestone thresholds** for permanent multipliers (typical: every 25, 50, 100, 200, 300 owned → +2× or +3×)
- **Auto-generation rate** of lower tier by upper tier (governs derivative depth)
